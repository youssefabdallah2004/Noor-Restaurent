About

What About Me

My name is Nour, I was born in 1980. I'm from Sharqia Governorate. I have three children: Malak, Mohamed, and Maryam. I learned this profession from my wife's mother, Om Tamer. My restaurant is distinguished by the quality of its food and competitive prices, and my dream is to open many branches.

What About Om Tamer

Om Tamer was a well-known woman in her neighborhood, famous for her small ful and ta'meya cart on the corner of the street. Every morning before sunrise, she would prepare her pots of ful, mixing spices by memory, not by recipe. People said her ta'meya had a special taste because she cooked with patience and love. Om Tamer knew almost everyone by name, and she always asked about their families while wrapping sandwiches in brown paper. Even on busy days, she never rushed anyone. What About Staff

Om Tamer was a well-known woman in her neighborhood, famous for her ful and ta'meya business. What started as a small street cart slowly grew into a busy place with a large staff working under her supervision. She had cooks preparing the ful, others frying ta'meya, and a few workers handling orders and serving customers. Om Tamer was very strict but fair. She believed that teamwork and discipline were the secret behind her success. Even with many employees, she made sure the food quality never changed. To everyone who worked with her, she was not just a boss, but a leader who taught them how to work hard and respect customers.
